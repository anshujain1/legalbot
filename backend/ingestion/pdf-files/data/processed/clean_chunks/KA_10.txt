Aiming to cement its position as a science and technology powerhouse, Karnataka has proposed multiple initiatives aimed at fostering artificial intelligence, quantum research and sensor technology. The chief minister said the govt will invest Rs 50 crore over the next five years to establish the Centre for Applied AI for Tech Solutions (CATS). This hub aims to drive cutting-edge AI advancements. The chief minister also announced that Quantum Research Park(QuRP) Phase-2 will be set up with a grant of Rs 48 crore over the next three years in collaboration with the Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru. The Quantum Research Park, which is already functional, encourages scientific inventions and innovations in the field of quantum computing and related technologies. QuRP will also foster skill development, mentorship, and industry/startup collaborations in the domain of quantum technologies.Phase 2 of the park is expected to play a crucial role in propelling India's quantum ambitions forward. To make science more accessible at the grassroots level, new sub-regional science centres will be set up in Kolar, Ramanagara, Chitradurga and Vijayanagara with Rs 9 crore allocated for this year alone. These centres aim to nurture curiosity and innovation among young minds across the state. In a move that strengthens Karnataka's industrial R&D ecosystem, the govt said it will establish the SensorTech Innovation Hub at a cost of Rs 99 crore.
