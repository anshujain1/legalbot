Karnataka's IT and Biotechnology Minister, Priyank Kharge, has expressed confidence that the disruptions caused by artificial intelligence (AI) will be brief, with new job opportunities emerging. The state is implementing a large-scale reskilling initiative, Nipuna Karnataka, to prepare its talent pool for new-age technologies. The program aims to train over 500,000 individuals in areas such as AI, cybersecurity, and digital forensics, focusing on both local and global ecosystems [1]. Nipuna Karnataka, a Rs 300 crore initiative, is industry-driven and industry-focused. It aims to train talent at scale and is expected to skill individuals in key areas like artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, digital forensics, and other technologies in line with global tech demands. The state government aims to scale over 500,000 people in the coming financial year in these areas [1]. Karnataka retains a strong lead in technology, as evidenced by its IT exports, startups, and GCC presence. The state has attracted significant real estate investment, with 20,000 startups registered and over 45 unicorns based in

Bengaluru. Karnataka contributes 21% of the national bioeconomy and 65% of defense electronics manufacturing [1]. In addition, the state is addressing the growing demand for AI-literate professionals. IITM Pravartak Technologies Foundation, the Technology Innovation Hub of IIT Madras, has launched the Advanced Certificate in Applied Artificial Intelligence & Deep Learning. This 7-month, live online program is designed to equip aspiring professionals with cutting-edge skills in machine learning, deep learning frameworks, data analytics, and emerging AI technologies [2]. The global AI market is projected to grow from USD 189 billion in 2023 to USD 4.8 trillion by 2033. While automation poses challenges, AI can enhance productivity and complement human capabilities. In India, the need for one million AI professionals by 2026 highlights the importance of initiatives like Nipuna Karnataka and the IITM Pravartak program [2].
