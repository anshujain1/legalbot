In a bold step towards technological advancement, Kerala has unveiled an ambitious AI-focused policy aimed at propelling the state into a global hub for Generation AI. Embracing the transformative potential of artificial intelligence, Kerala’s new initiative promises to ignite innovation across sectors, positioning the state as a beacon of cutting-edge technology in India. Kerala has recently introduced a comprehensive AI policy aimed at transforming the state into a global hub for generative artificial intelligence (Gen AI). The policy includes several initiatives: - AI Cluster: Establishing an AI Cluster-based Industrial Park on a Public-Private Partnership basis. - Financial Assistance: Providing financial support to AI startups, including share capital investments and scale-up support. - Infrastructure Development: Creating common infrastructure such as Graphics Processing Centres and Global Capability Centres. - Adoption in Various Sectors: Promoting AI adoption in sectors like marine genome sequencing, tourism, healthcare, and IT. These steps are part of Kerala’s strategic push to enhance its AI ecosystem, attract investment, and create job opportunities. This policy not only underscores Kerala’s commitment to fostering a robust AI ecosystem but also signals its readiness to attract global investments and talent, paving the way for groundbreaking developments in healthcare, education, and beyond. Minister Rajeev’s AI Declaration outlined a comprehensive road map for Kerala’s transformation into an AI powerhouse. The Kerala State Industrial Development Corporation (KSIDC) will offer preferential share capital investments of up to Rs 5 crore to AI entities with a minimum investment of Rs 10 crore. Moreover, scale-up support of Rs 1 crore will be provided, along with other incentives detailed in the Industrial Policy. The fast-growing MSME sector in Kerala will also benefit from AI tools and financial incentives, strengthening AI entities across the state. Fostering Collaborative Efforts To foster collaboration in the AI sector, Kerala plans to form technology groups. These groups will include representatives from AI entities, research institutions, associations, and government agencies. Their aim is to facilitate partnerships and promote AI adoption across key sectors. Focus areas will include marine genome sequencing, tourism, healthcare, and IT/ITeS industries. The initiative aims to leverage AI for innovative solutions and economic growth in Kerala. The policy also emphasizes the adoption of AI to enhance governance, integrating
